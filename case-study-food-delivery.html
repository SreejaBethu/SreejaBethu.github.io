Food Delivery Mobile Application

Designing a seamless and enjoyable experience for ordering food on the go.

Overview

This project involved designing a mobile application that allows users to browse restaurants, order food, and track their deliveries. The primary goal was to create an intuitive and efficient user experience, addressing common pain points in existing food delivery services.

Role: UX/UI Designer, Researcher

Tools: Figma, Miro, Adobe Photoshop

Timeline: 6 weeks

Problem Statement

Users often face challenges with existing food delivery apps, such as:

  • Difficulty in finding specific cuisines or restaurants.
  • Confusing navigation and checkout processes.
  • Lack of clear delivery status updates.
  • Limited personalization options.

My Process

My design process followed a user-centered design approach:

Discover

User interviews, competitive analysis, persona creation.

Define

Problem statements, user flows, information architecture.

Develop

Wireframing, prototyping, visual design, design system.

Deliver

Usability testing, iteration, final handoff.

Research & Analysis

User Interviews

Conducted interviews with 10 potential users to understand their habits, motivations, and frustrations with food delivery apps. Key findings included:

  • Desire for clear estimated delivery times.
  • Preference for easy reordering of past meals.
  • Frustration with overly complex menu navigation.

Competitive Analysis

Analyzed leading food delivery apps to identify industry best practices and areas for differentiation.

Information Architecture & User Flows

Based on research, I developed a sitemap and user flows to outline the application's structure and the user's journey through key tasks, such as browsing, ordering, and tracking.

Wireframing & Prototyping

Started with low-fidelity wireframes to quickly explore layout options, then moved to mid-fidelity prototypes for testing core interactions.

High-fidelity prototypes were created in Figma, incorporating visual design elements and microinteractions.

Visual Design & UI Elements

The visual design aimed for a clean, modern, and appetizing aesthetic, using a warm color palette with intuitive iconography.

Usability Testing & Iterations

Conducted usability tests with 5 participants. Key feedback led to the following iterations:

  • Improved visibility of dietary filters.
  • Simplified the address input process.
  • Enhanced order confirmation screen with more delivery details.

Results & Learnings

Key Takeaways:

  • The importance of clear and concise information hierarchy, especially for complex menus.
  • Small microinteractions significantly improve user satisfaction.
  • Continuous user feedback is vital for refining the product.

The final design provided a streamlined and enjoyable food ordering experience, addressing the initial pain points identified during research.

Next Steps

  • Implement a robust rating and review system.
  • Explore integration with loyalty programs.
  • Expand personalization features based on past orders and preferences.
